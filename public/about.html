About Our Club

Learn about our mission, values, and the team behind Campus Cats APA Club.

The Campus Cats Animal Protection Association (APA) Club was founded by a group of students who noticed a growing population of stray and community cats on campus. Concerned about their well-being, we organized to provide regular feeding, medical care, and safe shelter for these animals.

Our mission is simple: to ensure that every cat on campus lives a safe, healthy, and dignified life. We work closely with local veterinary clinics, animal shelters, and the university administration to implement humane management strategies including our active Trap-Neuter-Return (TNR) program.

Beyond direct care, we educate the campus community about responsible interaction with stray animals, advocate for animal-friendly policies, and organize events that bring students together over a shared love for our feline friends. Whether you are a cat enthusiast, a future veterinarian, or just someone who wants to make a positive impact, there is a place for you in our club.
